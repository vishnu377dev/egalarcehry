1. The Science of Savings: K10C DualJet Engine

Why does the Celerio give so much mileage? It’s not luck; it’s Japanese engineering. The 2026 Celerio uses the 1.0L K10C engine. This engine has two fuel injectors for every cylinder instead of one. This is called DualJet technology.

In simple English, it means the fuel is sprayed into the engine like a fine mist. This mist burns completely, leaving no waste. Every drop of petrol is converted into motion. This is why the Celerio achieved a record-breaking 26.68 kmpl in ARAI testing. Whether you are in 1st gear in traffic or 5th gear on the highway, this engine is always in 'Saving Mode'.

VishTech Tip: If you drive mostly in heavy city traffic, choose the AGS (Automatic) variant. Surprisingly, it gives more mileage than the manual because the computer shifts gears at the perfect time to save fuel.

2. Design: Small on the Outside, Big on the Inside

Maruti has used the '3D Sculpted' design language for the Celerio. It has rounded corners and a smiling face. While it looks compact, which is great for parking in narrow Indian streets, the inside is a different story.

The wheelbase is long, which means more space between the front and back wheels. This gives the passengers massive legroom. Even a 6-foot-tall person can sit comfortably in the back. The boot space (Dickey) is 313 Liters—which is enough for two large suitcases and a few bags. For a small family, this is the perfect holiday car.

3. Interior & Connectivity: High-Tech on a Budget

The 2026 Celerio dashboard is a "Center Focused" design. The black theme with silver accents looks premium. The main attraction is the 7-inch SmartPlay Studio touchscreen.

  • Wireless Android Auto & Apple CarPlay (No more cables!)
  • Steering Mounted Audio Controls (Safety first)
  • Push Button Start/Stop with Smart Key
  • Polluted Air Filter (Keeping your family healthy)
  • Door Ajar Warning and Low Fuel Alert

4. The S-CNG Revolution: Running Cost of ₹3/km?

The Celerio S-CNG is the most searched variant on VishTech Cars. Maruti uses dual interdependent ECUs (computers) for the CNG system. This ensures that the car doesn't lose power even when the CNG tank is half empty.

With a mileage of 31.59 km/kg, your running cost is roughly ₹3 per kilometer. If you use a bike, it costs you ₹2.5 per km. Think about it—you can travel in an AC car with your family for almost the same price as a motorcycle! This is why the Celerio CNG is the king of Indian roads.

🔥 The Battle: Celerio vs Tata Tiago vs WagonR

Maruti WagonR: The "Tall Boy" has more headroom, but it feels unstable on highways due to its height.

Tata Tiago: It has a 4-star safety rating and a heavier body. However, the maintenance is higher and the mileage is much lower than the Celerio.

The Winner: The Celerio 2026 offers the best balance. It is safer than the WagonR and more fuel-efficient than the Tiago. It’s the "Goldilocks" of hatchbacks—just right.

5. Safety: Built on the HEARTECT Platform

Safety is no longer an option; it’s a necessity. The Celerio is built on Maruti’s HEARTECT platform, which uses ultra-high-tensile steel. In the event of a crash, the platform is designed to absorb the impact and keep the passenger cabin safe.

You also get:

  • Dual Airbags (Standard)
  • ABS with EBD (Prevents skidding during sudden braking)
  • Hill Hold Assist: This is a life-saver for new drivers. When you stop on a bridge or a slope, the car won't roll back for 3 seconds, giving you time to move your foot to the accelerator.
