MG Windsor EV Review 2026: Is it a Car or a Private Jet Lounge on Wheels?

By: VishTech Editorial Team Published: March 19, 2026
"Last Sunday, I took my parents out for a drive in the MG Windsor EV. My father, who has always complained about legroom in every car we've owned, sat in the back, reclined the 'Aero-Lounge' seat to 135 degrees, and fell asleep within ten minutes.

For him, it wasn't about the 0-100 speed or the battery capacity. It was about the silence and the comfort that made him feel like he was sitting in his favorite armchair at home. That’s when I realized—the Windsor EV isn't just an electric car; it's a gift of comfort for the people you love. In a world of aggressive SUVs, the Windsor is a calm, luxurious bubble."

The MG Windsor EV has shaken the Indian market in 2026. It’s not quite a hatchback, not quite an SUV, and not quite an MPV. MG calls it a 'CUV' (Crossover Utility Vehicle). But what really makes it stand out is how MG has priced it using the revolutionary BaaS model.

1. The BaaS Concept: Buying a Car without the Battery?

What is BaaS (Battery as a Service)?

In 2026, MG allows you to buy the Windsor EV at a much lower price (around ₹13.50 Lakh) because you don't own the battery. Instead, you pay a rental of roughly ₹3.5 per kilometer. This lowers the upfront cost and removes the worry of battery degradation. It’s like paying for fuel, but for your battery!

2. Interior: The "Business Class" Experience

The biggest USP of the Windsor EV is its cabin. There are no physical buttons! Everything is controlled by a massive 15.6-inch Grandview Touchscreen. The seats are draped in 'Quilted Italian Leatherette' and the rear seats offer a 135-degree recline, which is unheard of in this segment. With the Infinity View Glass Roof, the cabin feels airy, bright, and incredibly premium.

Technical & Battery Specs:
  • 🔋 Battery: 38 kWh Li-ion Pack
  • 🛣️ Range: 331 KM (ARAI)
  • ⚡ Charging: 0-80% in 55 mins (DC)
  • 🚀 Performance: 136 PS & 200 Nm Torque
  • 🛡️ Safety: 4-Star Expected (6 Airbags Std)
  • 📺 Screen: 15.6-inch Infotainment

3. Performance: Smooth and Silent

Driving the Windsor EV is a therapeutic experience. The 136 PS motor is not meant for drag racing, but it provides a very linear and smooth power delivery. In city traffic, the One-Pedal driving mode works beautifully. On highways, the car feels planted, although the soft suspension—which is great for comfort—does mean there is a bit of body roll in sharp corners.

4. Range and Charging

With a 38 kWh battery, the real-world range sits around 250-280 KM. For city dwellers and weekend trippers, this is more than enough. If you have a 7.4kW home charger, you can top it up overnight. For long trips, MG’s widespread charging network (eHUB) makes life much easier in 2026.

5. Windsor EV vs The Competition

Windsor EV vs Tata Nexon.ev: The Nexon feels more like a traditional SUV and has more ground clearance, but the Windsor wipes the floor with the Nexon when it comes to interior space and rear-seat comfort.

Windsor EV vs Tata Curvv.ev: The Curvv.ev offers a bigger battery and more range, but it is also much more expensive. The Windsor is for those who want luxury at a budget.

6. Pros and Cons

Pros (Why Buy?)
  • Unmatched rear-seat comfort (Lounge Seats).
  • Massive 15.6-inch screen with smooth UI.
  • BaaS model makes it very affordable upfront.
  • Extremely spacious cabin and 604L boot space.
Cons (Things to Consider)
  • No physical buttons (Can be distracting).
  • Ground clearance is lower than typical SUVs.
  • Real-world range is limited to ~260 KM.

Final Verdict: Who is this for?

The MG Windsor EV 2026 is for the urban family man. It’s for the person who values his family's comfort over rugged off-roading. If you want a car that feels like a ₹50 Lakh luxury sedan inside but costs only ₹15 Lakh, the Windsor EV is your only choice. It’s a bold, smart, and futuristic way to travel.
